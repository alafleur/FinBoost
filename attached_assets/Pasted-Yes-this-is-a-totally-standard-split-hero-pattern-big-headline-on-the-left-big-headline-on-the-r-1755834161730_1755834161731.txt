Yes—this is a totally standard “split hero” pattern: big headline on the left, big headline on the right, with a clean, minimal element in the middle. You don’t need a phone mockup or a video to make it work.

Here’s a **drop-in, production-safe** version that:

* Puts **“Learn Real Finance”** on the left and **“Earn Real Cash”** on the right
* Uses a **subtle vertical divider** in the middle (no placeholders or fake data)
* Stacks gracefully on mobile (centered, single column)
* Uses only **static Tailwind classes** (safe with JIT/purge)

---

### `HeroSplit.tsx`

```tsx
import React from "react";

export default function HeroSplit() {
  return (
    <section className="bg-white">
      <div className="mx-auto max-w-7xl px-6">
        {/* Top area with split headings */}
        <div className="pt-16 sm:pt-20 md:pt-24 lg:pt-28">
          <div className="grid grid-cols-1 items-center gap-10 lg:grid-cols-[1fr_auto_1fr]">
            {/* Left Headline */}
            <h1 className="text-left font-extrabold leading-[1.05] tracking-tight text-slate-900
                           text-4xl sm:text-5xl md:text-6xl lg:text-7xl">
              Learn Real Finance
            </h1>

            {/* Middle divider (kept intentionally minimal & premium) */}
            <div className="hidden lg:flex flex-col items-center">
              <div className="h-48 w-px rounded-full bg-gradient-to-b from-indigo-400/50 via-slate-300 to-blue-400/50" />
            </div>

            {/* Right Headline */}
            <h2 className="text-right font-extrabold leading-[1.05] tracking-tight text-slate-900
                           text-4xl sm:text-5xl md:text-6xl lg:text-7xl">
              Earn Real Cash
            </h2>
          </div>

          {/* Subhead (centered, authentic, no synthetic numbers) */}
          <p className="mx-auto mt-8 max-w-3xl text-center text-base sm:text-lg leading-relaxed text-slate-600">
            Short lessons and real-world actions earn{" "}
            <span className="font-semibold text-slate-800">tickets</span> for cash drawings — free to join.

              href="/auth?mode=signup"
              className="inline-flex h-12 items-center justify-center rounded-lg px-6
                         font-semibold text-white bg-blue-600 hover:bg-blue-700
                         focus-visible:outline-none focus-visible:ring-2 focus-visible:ring-blue-500"

    </section>

```

#### Why this works

* **Clear split**: Strong, confident left/right headlines with a **neutral center divider** (no mockups, no placeholders).
* **Professional rhythm**: Balanced spacing (`pt-16/20/24/28`, `mt-8`) and tight leading on headlines.
* **Mobile-first**: On small screens it stacks to a single centered headline (no awkward layout).
* **Future-proof**: When you *do* have a video or visual, drop it into the middle column (replace the divider `div` with your media at `lg+` only).

#### Optional tweaks (one-liners)

* Make it **left-aligned overall** (more Underdog-like):

  * Change the subhead & CTAs wrappers from `text-center` / `justify-center` to `text-left` / `justify-start` and add `mx-0` instead of `mx-auto`.
* Tighten letter-spacing slightly for a more “sportsbook” tone:

  * Add `tracking-tight md:tracking-tighter` to the headline classes.
* Dark primary:

  * Change button `bg-blue-600` → `bg-slate-900 hover:bg-slate-800`.

If you want the divider a bit more pronounced, increase its height (`h-64`) or make it solid (`bg-slate-300`) instead of gradient.
