You’re absolutely right—no synthetic numbers, no visual clutter, and a calmer rhythm. Below are two **refined, production-safe hero components** that address your feedback:

* **HeroMinimal** – cleanest version (no badges, no numbers)
* **HeroMinimalBadges** – optional *generic* badges (no numbers, no placeholders)

Both are:

* **single H1** (two lines), strong hierarchy
* **balanced vertical rhythm** (carefully tuned spacing)
* **static Tailwind classes only** (safe with JIT/purge)
* **mobile-first** with sensible `clamp()` for headings
* **competition-focused** and confident without trying too hard

## Swap-in instructions

1. Put one of the files above in `components/`.
2. In your `HomeV3.tsx`, import and render it at the top:

```tsx
import HeroMinimal from "@/components/HeroMinimal";
// or
import HeroMinimalBadges from "@/components/HeroMinimalBadges";

// ...
<HeroMinimal />
// or
// <HeroMinimalBadges />
```

---

## Notes on spacing & hierarchy

* **Headline** uses `leading-[1.08]` to keep the two lines tight and authoritative.
* **Subhead** uses `mt-6` and `max-w-2xl`—keeps it readable and calm.
* **Badges (if any)**: `mt-5` (slightly closer to subhead than CTAs), small size (`text-sm`) and subtle border; remove anytime.
* **CTAs**: `mt-8`, consistent button height `h-12`, no icons (quieter, more confident).
* **Legal**: `mt-5`, `text-xs sm:text-sm`, subdued.

---

If you want it even *more* Underdog-like, you can **drop the badges entirely** (use `HeroMinimal`) and slightly reduce the subhead size by replacing `text-[clamp(1rem,2.2vw,1.125rem)]` with `text-[clamp(1rem,1.8vw,1.1rem)]`—that makes the H1 feel even more dominant.

This should give you the calm, confident, competition-focused hero without any synthetic details.
