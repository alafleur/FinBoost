1. There’s still **rotation** on desktop (and a latent rotate utility on mobile’s wrapper), which forces antialiased resampling.&#x20;
2. There’s a **gloss overlay + ring/shadow** stack over the screenshot that softens edges.&#x20;

Your `DeviceScreenshot` already notes that rotation introduces interpolation; let’s follow that advice and also remove the extra effects.&#x20;

# What to ask Replit to do (minimal changes)

**A. Remove all rotation on the phone image**

* In `HeroSplit.tsx` delete `rotate-[8deg]` and `lg:rotate-[8deg]` on the phone containers (both desktop and mobile blocks).
  *Why:* any transform (even small) forces antialiasing and softens the bitmap.

**B. Remove the softening layers**

* Remove `shadow-*`, `ring-*`, and the two absolute gloss/glow overlays that sit above the `<img>` in both desktop and mobile hero.
  *Why:* these semi-transparent layers reduce perceived sharpness.

**C. Don’t force “crisp-edges”**

* In the mobile `<img>` remove `[image-rendering:crisp-edges]`. It can cause odd pixel snapping on some browsers. Let the browser use the native sampling with a correct 1×/2×/3× `srcSet`.

**D. Keep exact widths and your 1×/2×/3× assets**

* Keep `w-[240px]` (mobile) and `min-[390px]:w-[304px]` / `md:w-[304px]` and keep the `srcSet` entries that match those sizes.

That alone typically yields the biggest clarity jump.

### Why this helps (quick)

* No transforms → no antialiasing → **crisper edges**
* No semi-transparent overlays → **no softening veil**
* Exact 1×/2×/3× assets at exact CSS widths → **no browser resampling**

If you prefer to keep the angled look on desktop later, you can restore the `rotate-[8deg]` only on `lg:` and accept a tiny sharpness trade-off there while keeping mobile perfectly crisp.
